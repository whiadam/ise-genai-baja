USABILITY TEST — Unit 4 Project
Adam White | ise-genai-baja (Baja Campus Events App)

---

## Intro

My name is Adam White and I'm part of the team working on the Baja Campus Events App. The app is a Streamlit-based tool that helps students find and keep track of campus events. I asked two people to help test two new features we are planning to add. I told them they were not being graded, just trying to help us figure out if the features made sense. I told them to say out loud what they were thinking as they went through the paper prototype so I could understand what was going through their head. I gave them a made-up persona to think from while doing the tasks.

Participant 1: A college student who uses their phone a lot to find things to do on campus.
Participant 2: A college student who mostly just goes to class and doesn't really follow campus events that much.

---

## Tasks

### Feature 1 — Event Popularity Leaderboard

Persona: You're a student looking for something to do this weekend and want to see what events other people on campus are actually going to.

Task: Find out which campus event is getting the most attention right now and look into it more.

### Feature 2 — Event Bookmarking and Saved Events

Persona: You found a few events earlier in the week that you want to remember. You're back on the app and want to find them again without looking through the full list.

Task: Find a way to save an event you're interested in so you can come back to it later.

## Results

### Issues Found

Issue 1: Event cards on the leaderboard didn't look obviously tappable
- Hypothesis: The cards didn't have any visual cue like an arrow or underline to suggest they were interactive
- Participant quote: "I didn't know the list was clickable at first, maybe make it more obvious"
- Remedy: Add a small arrow icon or a subtle highlight effect on hover to show the cards are clickable

Issue 2: Trending arrows were confusing
- Hypothesis: The arrows alone didn't have enough context, users weren't sure if they meant popularity, views, or something else
- Participant quote: "I wasn't sure what the arrows meant, like is that popularity or something else"
- Remedy: Add a short label next to the arrow like "Trending up this week" or a small percentage number so the meaning is clear

Issue 3: Star icon purpose wasn't obvious before tapping
- Hypothesis: A plain star with no label looks decorative, not functional, especially if users haven't seen it before
- Participant quote: "I didn't know what it was for before I tapped it"
- Remedy: Add a small tooltip or label on hover that says "Save event" so users know what it does before they tap

Issue 4: Saved Events tab was hard to find
- Hypothesis: The tab label probably looked similar to the other tabs and didn't stand out enough as a personal/saved section
- Participant quote: "I didn't know where my saved stuff went, I had to look around for it"
- Remedy: Rename the tab to something more personal like "Saved" with a bookmark icon so it's easier to spot
